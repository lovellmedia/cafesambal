# Master Research Topics for cafesambal.com

## Topic 1: Regional Sambal Traditions Across Maritime Southeast Asia
- Slug: regional-sambal-traditions
- Description: A comparative site about how sambal changes across Indonesia, Malaysia, and Singapore, with emphasis on ingredients, grinding methods, fermentation, and household use.
- Why Underserved: Most recipe sites publish one sambal formula at a time rather than explaining how regional traditions differ or why ingredients shift by place.
- Pillar 1 (Overview): What sambal is, how it works as a condiment and cooking base, and why regionality matters.
- Pillar 2 (History): Pre-chili condiments, chili adoption after Iberian trade, and the spread of sambal across ports and islands.
- Pillar 3 (Technical): Ingredient functions, mortar versus blender texture, oil handling, acidity, salt, and storage.
- Pillar 4 (Ontology): Taxonomy of fresh, cooked, fermented, sweet, citrus-based, and seafood-forward sambals.
- Pillar 5 (Trends): Restaurant revival, jarred sambal brands, diaspora adaptations, and recipe-video culture.
- Pillar 6 (Tools): Heat-balancing calculator, pantry substitution guide, batch-scaling helper.
- Pillar 7 (Challenges): Bitterness, harsh raw chili flavor, oily separation, oversalting, and short shelf life.
- Sources: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sambal ; https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Varieties_of_sambal ; https://journalofethnicfoods.biomedcentral.com/articles/10.1186/s42779-022-00142-7
